How to Choose Your Perfect Breed

Important factors to consider when selecting a dog breed for your family

Consider Your Lifestyle

+

Your daily routine and activity level should match your dog's needs. Active families who enjoy outdoor activities might prefer high-energy breeds like Golden Retrievers or German Shepherds. If you have a more relaxed lifestyle or live in an apartment, consider lower-energy breeds like French Bulldogs.

Key Questions:
• How many hours per day can you dedicate to exercise?
• Do you work from home or have someone home during the day?
• Are you an active outdoor person or prefer staying indoors?

Space Requirements

+

Living space is crucial for your dog's wellbeing. Large breeds like Caucasian Shepherds need significant space and are better suited to homes with yards. Small to medium breeds like French Bulldogs or American Bullies can adapt well to apartment living with proper exercise.

Space Guidelines:
• Small breeds (up to 25 lbs): Apartments or small homes
• Medium breeds (25-60 lbs): Medium homes with some outdoor access
• Large breeds (60+ lbs): Large homes with yards preferred

Activity Level Match

+

Different breeds have vastly different exercise needs. High-energy breeds like Siberian Huskies need 2+ hours of vigorous exercise daily, while breeds like French Bulldogs are satisfied with shorter, moderate walks. Matching energy levels prevents behavioral problems and ensures happiness for both you and your dog.

Exercise Requirements:
• Low energy: 30-45 minutes daily (French Bulldog)
• Medium energy: 1-1.5 hours daily (Rottweiler)
• High energy: 2+ hours daily (German Shepherd, Husky)

Grooming Commitment

+

Grooming needs vary significantly by breed. Some dogs require professional grooming every 6-8 weeks, while others need only occasional brushing. Long-haired breeds like Caucasian Shepherds require daily brushing, while short-haired breeds like Labradors are low-maintenance.

Grooming Levels:
• Low: Weekly brushing, occasional baths (Labrador, Rottweiler)
• Medium: Twice-weekly brushing, monthly grooming (Golden Retriever)
• High: Daily brushing, professional grooming (Caucasian Shepherd, Husky)

Training Needs

+

All dogs need training, but some breeds are easier than others. First-time owners might prefer highly trainable breeds like Golden Retrievers or Labradors. Independent thinkers like Huskies or protective breeds like Caucasian Shepherds require experienced handlers and consistent training.

Training Difficulty:
• Beginner-friendly: Golden Retriever, Labrador (eager to please)
• Intermediate: German Shepherd, Rottweiler (needs consistency)
• Advanced: Husky, Caucasian Shepherd (independent, strong-willed)

Cost of Ownership

+

Beyond the initial purchase price, consider ongoing costs: food (larger dogs eat more), veterinary care, grooming, training, supplies, and insurance. Large breeds can cost ₦30,000-50,000 monthly, while small breeds may cost ₦15,000-25,000 monthly in Nigeria.

Monthly Cost Estimates (Lagos):
• Small breeds: ₦15,000-25,000 (food, basic care)
• Medium breeds: ₦20,000-35,000
• Large breeds: ₦30,000-50,000+
• Additional: Vet visits, vaccinations, emergencies

Health Considerations

+

Different breeds have different health predispositions. Large breeds may develop hip dysplasia, while flat-faced breeds like French Bulldogs can have breathing issues. Understanding breed-specific health concerns helps you prepare for potential veterinary needs and costs.

Common Health Considerations:
• Large breeds: Joint issues, bloat prevention
• Small breeds: Dental care, weight management
• All breeds: Regular vaccinations, parasite prevention
• Choose breeders who health-test breeding dogs
